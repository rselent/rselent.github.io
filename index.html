What is data science?

Data science is a broad, interdisciplinary field that blends data inference, algorithm development, and scientific methods to extract knowledge, patterns, questions, and insights from both structured and unstructured data, big and small.
Using everything from statistical analysis to machine learning, data science encourages us to discover and understand the hows and whys of data sets that aren't readily apparent.

What do data scientists do?

In 2012, IBM calculated that every day, 2.5 exabytes of data (one exabyte is one thousand thousand terabytes) are generated. The availability of all of that data has both altered business models of old industries and enabled the creation of new ones, according to Forbes. And according to Oracle, data-driven businesses are now worth $1.2 trillion in 2020, up from $333 million in 2015.

Data scientists like me are responsible for breaking down that data into digestible, actionable information and helping make that growth happen.
