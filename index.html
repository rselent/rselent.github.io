What is data science?

Data science is a broad, interdisciplinary field that blends data inference, algorithm development, and scientific methods to extract knowledge, patterns, questions, and insights from data, big and small.
Using everything from statistical analysis to machine learning and artificial intelligence, data science encourages us to discover and understand the hows & whys of the data that's produced by our modern world – making everything from recommendations on Netflix, to Siri, self-driving cars, and better hurricane forecasting, possible.

What do data scientists do?

In 2012, IBM estimated that every day, 2.5 exabytes of data are generated.
(1 exabyte = 1,000,000 terabytes.)
According to Forbes and Oracle, the availability of all that daily data has simultaneously altered business models of old industries and enabled the creation of new ones. Data-driven businesses were worth $1.2 trillion in 2020, up from $333 million only 5 years earlier.

Data scientists break down those exabytes of data into digestible, actionable bits of information... and then use it to transform our modern world.
